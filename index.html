About Us

The Wildlife Conservation Society saves wildlife and wild places around the world. WCS runs four zoos and an aquarium in New York and has global conservation programs in more than 50 countries. WCS Organization is a forward-thinking entity dedicated to building strong partnerships and ensuring transparent collaboration with vendors and stakeholders.

Founded in 1895 as the New York Zoological Society, the Wildlife Conservation Society was one of the first conservation organizations in the U.S. The Society began with a clear mandate: advance wildlife conservation, promote the study of zoology, and create a first-class zoo. Today, WCS operates five major facilities including the Bronx Zoo and the New York Aquarium.

Our Mission

WCS saves wildlife and wild places worldwide through science, conservation action, education, and inspiring people to value nature.

Our Vision

WCS envisions a world where wildlife thrives in healthy lands and seas, valued by societies that embrace and benefit from their diversity.
